Verb Tense Lesson

SIMPLE PRESENT

We use the simple present to talk about routines, habits, facts, and things that are generally true.

⏰ Wake up 🪥 Brush teeth ☕ Have breakfast 🚌 Go to school 📚 Study 😴 Sleep

When To Use It

Simple and useful for everyday English

Wake up
Study
Work
Sleep
  • Daily routines: I wake up at 6:00.
  • Habits: She drinks coffee every morning.
  • Facts: Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
  • Likes and dislikes: They like pop music.
  • Negative: He does not like math.
  • Question: Do they live here?

Simple Present Chart

Affirmative, Negative, and Questions

Form
Structure
Examples
Affirmative
I / You / We / They + base verb
He / She / It + verb + s

PS: For verbs ending in -o, -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, or -z, we add -es.
For verbs ending in consonant + y, we replace the y with -ies.
I study English every day.
She works at a school.
He fixes cars.
My dog cries at night.
Negative
I / You / We / They + do not + base verb
He / She / It + does not + base verb
I do not study at night.
He does not like math.
Questions
Do + I / you / we / they + base verb?
Does + he / she / it + base verb?
Wh word + do / does + subject + base verb?
Do they live here? Yes, they do.
Does she teach English? No, she doesn't.
Where do you study? I study at Roosevelt School.
What does he eat for breakfast? He eats bread and fruit.
