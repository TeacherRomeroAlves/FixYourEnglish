Verb Tense Lesson

SIMPLE PAST

We use the simple past to talk about finished actions and completed events in the past.

📅 Yesterday 🌙 Last night 🎉 Last weekend ✅ Finished action

When To Use It

For actions that already finished

Yesterday
Last week
Ago
In 2024
  • Finished action: I visited my grandmother yesterday.
  • Past routine: She walked to school last year.
  • Negative: He did not watch TV last night.
  • Question: Did they play soccer after class?

Simple Past Chart

Affirmative, Negative, and Questions

Form
Structure
Examples
Affirmative
Subject + past form of the verb

PS: Regular verbs usually add -ed. Some verbs are irregular: go -> went, eat -> ate.
I visited my cousin yesterday.
She went to school by bus.
They studied for the test.
Negative
Subject + did not + base verb
I did not watch TV last night.
He did not play soccer yesterday.
Questions
Did + subject + base verb?
Wh word + did + subject + base verb?
Did they visit their uncle? Yes, they did.
Did she study last night? No, she didn't.
Where did you go yesterday? I went to the mall.
What did he eat for lunch? He ate pasta.
