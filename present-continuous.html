Verb Tense Lesson

PRESENT CONTINUOUS

We use the present continuous to talk about actions happening now or around this moment.

Created by Teacher Romero Alves Instagram and Teacher Lais Queiroz Instagram

Reading now Cooking now Studying now Talking now Working now Listening now

When To Use It

Useful for actions in progress

Now
At the moment
Today
This week
  • Action now: I am doing my homework now.
  • Temporary situation: She is staying with her aunt this week.
  • Negative: He is not sleeping right now.
  • Question: Are they studying for the test?

Present Continuous Chart

Affirmative, Negative, and Questions

Form
Structure
Examples
Affirmative
I + am + verb + ing
You / We / They + are + verb + ing
He / She / It + is + verb + ing

PS: Most verbs add -ing. Some verbs drop final -e: make -> making.
I am reading a book now.
They are playing outside.
She is making dinner.
Negative
Subject + am / is / are + not + verb + ing
I am not watching TV.
He is not working today.
Questions
Am / Is / Are + subject + verb + ing?
Wh word + am / is / are + subject + verb + ing?
Are they doing their homework? Yes, they are.
Is she studying now? No, she isn't.
What are you doing? I am doing my homework.
Where is he going? He is going to the library.
